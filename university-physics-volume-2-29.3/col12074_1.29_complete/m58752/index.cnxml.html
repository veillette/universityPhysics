A circuit with resistance and self-inductance is known as an RL circuit. [link](a) shows an RL circuit consisting of a resistor, an inductor, a constant source of emf, and switches S1S1 and S2.S2. When S1S1 is closed, the circuit is equivalent to a single-loop circuit consisting of a resistor and an inductor connected across a source of emf ([link](b)). When S1S1 is opened and S2S2 is closed, the circuit becomes a single-loop circuit with only a resistor and an inductor ([link](c)).

We first consider the RL circuit of [link](b). Once S1S1 is closed and S2S2 is open, the source of emf produces a current in the circuit. In accordance with Lenz’s law, the induced emf counteracts the increase in the current and is directed as shown in the figure. As a result, I(t) starts at zero and increases asymptotically to its final value.
